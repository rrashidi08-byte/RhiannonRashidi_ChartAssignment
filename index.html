NYCHA Needs Billions of Dollars for Repairs

This year, the New York City Housing Authority released a report showing they need over 61 billion dollars for repairs and maintenance across all of its developments.

By Rhiannon Rashidi

Nov. 18-- The New York City Housing Authority (NYCHA) needs over one bilion dollars in repairs across multiple work order categories like elevator repair, lead based paint removal, and safety and security infastructure. But its largest category of need is for apartment maintenance.

Within the apartment maintenance category, over five billion dollars is needed for kitchen repairs and over seven billion is needed for bathroom repairs. But renovations is the largest category, with over 12 billion dollars needed. Though NYCHA's Physical Needs Report doesn't specifiy what kind of renovations are needed, residents of NYCHA complexes report mold, holes in the ceilings, and rodent damage.

Susan J. Popkin is a fellow at the Urban Institue and leads its Future of Pupblic Housing Initiative. She said that a lack of funding coming into the Housing Authority from the federal government is to blame. "What you're dealing with in New York, we're in a situation where the funding for public housing is continuing to get squeezed. This is not the 1990s where there was the big infusion of cash so it's really important to have sustained civic engagement and sustained support," she said.

Community members say they have to be organized and on top of the housing authority if they want any work to be done on their units. Susan Perez, the newly re-elected tenant association president at the John Hayes Holmes Towers says one of her priotities is to advocate for residents to get their maintenance requests completed in a timely manner.
